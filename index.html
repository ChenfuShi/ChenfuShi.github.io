About me

I am a PhD student at the University of Manchester in the Wellcome trust Molecular and Cell Biology programme.
I am originally from Italy (although I don't look Italian), and I did my undergraduate degree at the University of Trento in Biomolecular Sciences and Technologies.
During my studies I developed an interest in Bioinformatics and genomics data science, which developed into my current research project with Gisela Orozco.

My current research

My project with Gisela Orozco involves dissecting the genetic role in the development of psoriatic arthritis and rheumatoid arthritis.
We use newly developed techniques such as capture HiC and HiChIP to study the 3d conformation of the chromosomes in the nucleus and link disease associated variants to affected genes in the hope of gaining a better understanding of the diseases.

My past research

I did my undergraduate degree at the University of Trento. I did my final project with Professor Massimo Pizzato.


I did my first rotation of the Wellcome trust programme in Adam Hurlstone's lab. I've investigated the possibility to use Chimeric antigen receptors(CARs) in the zebrafish model.
