About me

I am a PhD student from Italy currently studying at the University of Manchester in the 4yr Wellcome Trust PhD programme in Molecular and Cell Biology.
I did my undergraduate degree at the University of Trento in Biomolecular Sciences and Technologies.
During my studies I developed an interest in Bioinformatics and genomics data science, which developed into my current research project with Gisela Orozco.

My current research

In the last decade genetic studies (e.g. GWAS) have linked a large number of variants to complex diseases such as Psoriatic Arthritis. These variants, however, do not usually affect the coding sequence of proteins but rather non-coding regulatory regions often with unknown targets.
In my project I work as a Bioinformatician integrating functional genomics data such as RNA-seq, ChIP-seq and chromatin conformation to direct the search of target genes that are potentially influenced by disease associated variants.
In my work I have developed pipelines and software to process, analyse and integrate the available datasets.
Some of my work is available on GitHub

My past research

First rotation
I did my first rotation of the Wellcome trust programme in Adam Hurlstone's lab. I've investigated the possibility to use Chimeric antigen receptors(CARs) in the zebrafish model.

Second rotation
I did my second rotation in Dan Davis's lab, using Super Resolution Microscopy to study the spatial distribution of immune receptors on NK cells.

Undergraduate Thesis project
I did my final undergraduate project with Massimo Pizzato, Laboratory of Virus-Cell Interaction. My final thesis project was focused on "Cost-effective workflow for molecular and cellular basic laboratory applications".

Undergraduate summer internship
I worked as a summer internship student in Harvey McMahon's lab at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology in Cambridge. My work involved identifying protein-protein interactions in autophagy and Parkinson's disease related proteins via pulldowns through brain and mitochondria extracts with GST fused baits. The interactors were identified by mass spectrometry and then reproduced with different techniques. Finally, I have started the functional characterization of a newly discovered interaction by investigating their co-localization by confocal microscopy.
